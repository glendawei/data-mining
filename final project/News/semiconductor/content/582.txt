TSMC Plans Advanced $12bn Chip Plant in Arizona – WSJ The cutting-edge semiconductor plant will be capable of making advanced 3-nanometre computer chips, the Wall Street Journal said The cutting-edge semiconductor plant will be capable of making advanced 3-nanometre computer chips, the Wall Street Journal said  Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Co Ltd is planning a new multibillion-dollar investment in a factory in the US, the Wall Street Journal reported. The Taiwanese chip giant – the world’s largest chipmaker – will build a cutting-edge semiconductor plant to make 3nanometre chips in northern Phoenix, Arizona, according to the report, which said it would cost the world’s biggest chipmaker about $12 billion. The United States has been encouraging foreign tech firms to manufacture in the country and also vigorously supporting local research, development and manufacturing after passing its CHIPS Act.
