UK Delays Verdict On Chip Firm Sale to China – Guardian Business secretary Kwasi Kwarteng has put off a decision on whether to permit the sale of Newport Wafer Fab Business secretary Kwasi Kwarteng has put off a decision on whether to permit the sale of Newport Wafer Fab  The UK government will delay a ruling on whether China-owned firm Nexperia will be allowed to buy Britain’s largest chip manufacturer Newport Wafer Fab by a month and a half, The Guardian reports. The final verdict on the controversial £63 million buyout by Nexperia, a Dutch firm owned by China’s Wingtech, had been expected to be made by business secretary Kwasi Kwarteng on Tuesday, the report said. But national security concerns have now set the deal back. Read the full report: The Guardian.
