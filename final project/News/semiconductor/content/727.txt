Japan’s TDK Looks to Build $383m EV Parts Facility – Nikkei The new factory, which the company hopes to set up by 2024 in Kitakami in Iwate prefecture, will manufacture ceramic capacitors The new factory, which the company hopes to set up by 2024 in Kitakami in Iwate prefecture, will manufacture ceramic capacitors  Japanese electronics maker TDK plans to spend about 50 billion yen ($383 million) to build a factory that will make electric vehicle parts, Nikkei reported. The new factory, which the company hopes to set up by 2024 in Kitakami in Iwate prefecture, will manufacture ceramic capacitors, the report added.  Read the full report:  Nikkei Asia.
